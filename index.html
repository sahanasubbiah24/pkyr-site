Why This Matters

Your Rights Under
the Constitution

The Bill of Rights protects your fundamental freedoms from government overreach — but knowing about them and knowing how they work in practice are very different things.

Less than 40% of Americans can name all five First Amendment freedoms. PKYR is changing that.

Key Rights to Know

  • 1stSpeech, press, religion, assembly, petition
  • 4thProtection from unreasonable searches
  • 5thRight to remain silent
  • 6thRight to a fair trial
  • 26thVoting rights at 18

Choose Your Scenario

Real situations. Real rights. What would you do?

Why Scenarios?

Knowledge only sticks
when it's real

You can memorize the Bill of Rights — but when a police officer approaches you, what do you actually do? Our scenarios close the gap between knowing and acting.

Why are we asking?

Research consistently shows civic education is unequally distributed — students in lower-income communities receive far less instruction. Your response helps PKYR advocate for better policy.

Your Vote Is Your Voice. Use It.

Voting is the most direct way to influence your government. Here's everything you need before Election Day.

Register at vote.gov Absentee Voting

Am I Eligible?

U.S. citizens 18+ on Election Day. Many states allow pre-registration at 16–17.

How to Register

Online, by mail, or in person. Most states have deadlines 15–30 days out.

Voting as a Student

Register at your school or home address. Absentee voting lets you vote in your home district while away.

Election Day

Bring ID if required. If you're in line when polls close, stay — you have the right to vote.

Local Elections Matter Most

School board, city council, judges — these races happen every year and affect daily life most.

Help Others Vote

Once registered, help friends register too. Volunteer as a poll worker — you can get paid!
